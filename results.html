About This Implementation

TurboQuant (Zandieh, Daliri, Hadian & Mirrokni, Google Research, 2025) is a data-oblivious vector quantization algorithm that achieves near-optimal distortion rates within a ≈2.7× factor of the information-theoretic lower bound. It works by randomly rotating input vectors via an orthogonal matrix Π (QR decomposition of a Gaussian matrix) to induce a concentrated Beta distribution on each coordinate, then applying precomputed Lloyd-Max optimal scalar quantizers per coordinate. For unbiased inner-product estimation — critical for attention score accuracy — TurboQuant combines the MSE-optimal quantizer with a 1-bit Quantized Johnson–Lindenstrauss (QJL) transform on the residual. The paper demonstrates quality neutrality at 3.5 bits per channel and marginal degradation at 2.5 bits, with over 4× KV cache compression on Llama-3.1-8B and Ministral-7B.

Paper: arXiv:2504.19874 · QJL: arXiv:2406.03482 · PolarQuant: arXiv:2502.02617

Approximations vs. the Paper

  • GPT-2 Medium has d_head=64; the paper targets d_head=128 (Llama/Ministral). At d=64 the Beta distribution is still well-concentrated.
  • No outlier channel separation (paper assigns different bit-widths to outlier vs. non-outlier channels based on calibration).
  • No custom CUDA kernels — all quantization uses PyTorch ops applied between forward passes, not fused inside attention.
  • Codebook is computed via numerical Lloyd-Max on the exact Beta PDF with d=64 (not the Gaussian approximation).
